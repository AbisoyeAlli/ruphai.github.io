Summary

Rufai Omowunmi Balogun is a geospatial Analyst and a sustainability aficionado. He has a growing interest in solving tough Climate and Environmental Disaster Risks challenges using Big Earth Observation data, Artificial Intelligence and Spatial Analytics. Before now, he had worked as a Research Intern at the National Space Research and Development Agency (NASRDA), Nigeria. He also worked as a remote data intern at Hash Analytics and as a Geospatial Analyst at the Center for Space Research and Applications (CESRA), Akure, Ondo State, Nigeria. He is involved in Climate with Care About Climate as a Project Mentor and as an Online Youth Exchange, he has also served as a member of the International Youth Advisory Council of the World Oceans Day. He is also an Earth Charter Young Leader heralding the Earth Charter Principles for Sustainable Development. He has a Bachelors Degree in Meteorology and Climate Science from the Federal University of Technology, Akure, Ondo State, Nigeria. At the moment, he is pursuing a degree in Copernicus Master in Digital Earth funded by the Erasmus Mundus Plus Program in conjunction with the European Commission. He is also working along side other Early Career Scientists in building the Enhancing Adaptation and Resilience along the West African Coast (EARWAC) at the SixthAvis. When not rushing to meet school deadlines, he can be found playing board games or providing guidance on Public Speaking, Leadership and Grad School Applications.
